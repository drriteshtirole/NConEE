<div class="elementor-tab-title elementor-tab-mobile-title" aria-selected="false" data-tab="5" role="tab" tabindex="-1" aria-controls="elementor-tab-content-2455" aria-expanded="false">Author Guidelines</div>
<div id="elementor-tab-content-2455" class="elementor-tab-content elementor-clearfix" data-tab="5" role="tabpanel" aria-labelledby="elementor-tab-title-2455" tabindex="0" hidden="hidden"><strong>PUBLICATION AND REVIEW PROCESS</strong>
<br><br>
<strong>Full-Length Paper Publication</strong>
<br>
Taylor &amp; Francis (T&amp;F) is a globally recognized publishing enterprise dedicated to making
high-quality research accessible to individuals worldwide. By bridging diverse perspectives,
ideas, and expert insights, T&amp;F fosters the dissemination of theoretical frameworks and
empirical studies across various disciplines.

<br><br>
All full-length papers accepted for presentation at the ICIAS-2025 Conference will be
compiled in a conference proceeding published by Taylor &amp; Francis (T&amp;F). This proceeding
will be available in electronic formats, each assigned an ISBN for the book and a DOI for
each chapter. Additionally, T&amp;F will promote the published volume on its official website
and advocate for its consideration in Scopus indexing, provided it meets Scopuss selection
criteria, scope, and quality standards.
<br><br>

<strong>Abstract Publication</strong><br>
All accepted abstracts for oral and poster presentations will be published in the conference
souvenir cum abstract book, serving as a record of the innovative research presented at
ICIAS-2025.
<br><br>

<strong>Best Paper Award and Recognition</strong><br>
The conference will honor outstanding research contributions with the Best Paper Award and
Appreciation Certificates for both oral and poster presentations. This recognition aims to
encourage excellence in academic and industrial research.
<br><br>

<strong>Review Process</strong><br>
The editorial board will conduct a rigorous double-blind peer review process for all submitted
papers and abstracts. Each submission will be reviewed for originality, relevance, and quality.
Upon completion of the review process, authors will receive acceptance notifications along
with reviewers&#8217; comments for necessary revisions.
<br>
All accepted papers and abstracts must be presented at the conference, either in oral or poster
format, to ensure their inclusion in the conference proceedings.
<br><br>
<strong>PUBLICATION &amp; INDEXING</strong><br>
All accepted and presented papers will be published in online proceedings and indexed in
Scopus.
<br><br>
<strong>FULL LENGTH PAPER SUBMISSION</strong><br>
All full-length papers accepted at the ICIAS-2025 Conference will be compiled into a
conference proceeding published by Taylor &amp; Francis (T&amp;F) in electronic formats. Each
publication will have an ISBN for the book and a DOI for each chapter, accompanied by
promotional activities on the T&amp;F website. T&amp;F will advocate for the published volume to be
considered for indexing by Scopus, subject to compliance with Scopuss selection criteria and
fulfillment of the necessary scope and quality standards for Scopus Indexing.
<br><br>
Authors bear full responsibility for ensuring the originality, validity, and integrity of their
submissions. Adhering to ethical publication practices within the scientific community,
authors must refrain from using Generative AI tools in ways that replace essential research
and authorship responsibilities, including:
<br><br><ul>
<li> Generating text or code without thorough revision.</li>
<li>Creating synthetic data as a substitute for missing data without a sound
methodological basis.</li>
<li>Producing any content that is inaccurate, including abstracts or supplementary
materials.</li>
<li>Manipulating images, figures, or original research data.</li>
</ul>
<br>
The review process will evaluate submissions for both similarity/plagiarism and AI-generated
content. Papers exhibiting the following characteristics in text, figures, code, or other
elements will not be considered for full paper submission:
<li>Similarity/plagiarism: 10% or higher.</li>
<li>AI-generated content: 20% or higher.</li>
